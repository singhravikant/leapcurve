What is career level?

Career level is an empirical score arrived at after analyzing your responses to a set of questions related to your role, industry and the kind of organization you work for. It denotes the complexity of your role, the level of responsibility you handle and the complexity of the business of your organization.

Understanding Career Level helps you to understand a lot of important question:

  • - Where do I stand in my career with respect to the managerial level & role complexity?
  • - How does my compensation compare against my peers with similar career levels?
  • - Is my organization underpaying me vs. other companies?
  • - Which industries offer better compensation for my role?
  • - How difficult is it for me to improve my career level with in my organization and industry?
  • - Which industries should I target to improve my career level & compensation?

How do we arrive at career level score?

The 3 most integral factors that contribute to deriving your career level are:

  • - Areas of responsibility your role carries
  • - Impact of your role on your organization
  • - Complexity of the environment that your organization functions in.

How to read the career level score?

Every increase of 10 points on the score is a noticeable difference. For a career jump, a 50-point increase is considered ideal. Since many aspects feed into the career level score, your motive should be to improve the score on a certain aspect, which in turn will affect the overall career level.
